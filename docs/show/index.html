💥 Amazing Topics 💥

🧠 Thinking Amazing 🧠

For us to live amazing we'll need to come up with goals that would be amazing for all humanity. No goal is too big or too small. Bring goals for discussion even if you don't see how they could be achieved. Bring personal goals too, such as things you hope to do in your lifetime, including creative endeavors

🏘 Living Amazing 🏘

We'll discuss the metropolitan area the show is in to see how every aspect of living could be amazing including: traffic, safety, transportation, sanitation, working, bills, housing, learning, amusement, festivals, parades, architecture, parks, trees, nature, pets, animals, and more

🎙 Singing Amazing 🎙

Just how all birds of a species can sing equally beautiful, all beings of our species are capable of learning how to sing beautifully. Everyone will be able to sing using thier voice or using instruments such as guitars, pianos, drums, brass, strings, and more

💃 Moving Amazing 🕺

All humans of our species can learn to move beautifully including: babies, toddlers, children, adults, politicians, and more. Everyone will be able to beautifully dance to any song, play any sport, or do any other movement beautifully

🎩 Magic Amazing 🎩

At every discussion everyone will learn how to perform some magic including card tricks, getting objects to emerge from behind ears, and making anger and fear disapper from our homes and communities

The purpose of the show is to plan and build a world where life is amazing for all

LIKELY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I need to read the World Amazing plan before coming to a show?
No. You do not need to read anything in advance to attend. The show is open to anyone willing to come in good faith. If you want to look over the plan beforehand, that can help orient your thinking, but it is not a requirement.

Should I read the plan before coming anyway?
If you feel drawn to, yes. Reading some of the plan beforehand can help ready your mind for deeper work, broader thinking, and more grounded participation. But the show is also a place where people can encounter these ideas for the first time, in public, with others.

What kind of mindset should I bring?
Bring an open mind, real questions, and a willingness to think beyond the usual limits. You do not need polished opinions. You do not need credentials. You do not need to already know what the future should look like. You do need a willingness to consider that life could be made far better than what we have accepted.

What if I feel skeptical?
Skepticism is welcome. The point is not blind agreement. The point is to think seriously, test ideas honestly, and see what becomes visible when people stop treating the current world as the ceiling of what is possible.

What if I feel angry, grieving, ashamed, overwhelmed, or closed off?
You are not disqualified. Most people alive today have been shaped by a world that runs on stress, distortion, fear, blame, and survival habits. The show is not built on the assumption that people arrive fully healed. It is built on the understanding that humans can think, feel, move, speak, and gather their way toward something better.

Is this just discussion, or is it also relief?
It can be both. The show is a place for deep thought, but also for deep relief: the relief of saying what matters, hearing others think honestly, moving together, singing together, laughing, imagining better, and feeling for a moment what public life could be like in a healthier civilization.

Do I need to speak?
No. You are welcome to speak, ask questions, share ideas, sing, move, or participate more actively, but you do not need to perform. Some people will speak often. Some will mostly listen and take it in. Both are valid.

What kinds of things will people talk about?
Anything that matters to making life amazing for all: homes, work, health, learning, safety, beauty, childhood, nature, public life, transportation, music, movement, community, repair, and the many ways civilization could be made more sane, more cooperative, and more alive.

Is this only for people who already see this as the way forward?
No. The show is not only for people who already see it clearly. It is for people willing to engage honestly, think seriously, and look for themselves. Some will arrive already convinced that humanity needs a total shift. Others will arrive curious, cautious, skeptical, or uncertain. The point is not forced agreement. The point is to make the deeper reality easier to see in public.

Is this political?
Not in the narrow party sense. The show concerns public life, how humans live together, and what kind of civilization we are building. It reaches beneath ordinary politics into deeper questions of truth, care, housing, work, childhood, beauty, repair, and what life should actually be like.

What if I do not think of myself as a “show person”?
This is not about fitting a type. Some gatherings may feel more like a public conversation. Some may feel more like a walk, a shared inquiry, a movement-based gathering, or a civic celebration. The point is not to match a pre-existing culture. The point is to help build a better one.

Will every show be the same?
No. Different places, groups, and moments will shape different formats. Some shows may be seated. Some may be walking. Some may lean more heavily into discussion, singing, movement, or shared public space. What remains constant is the purpose: helping make life amazing for all.

Can children come?
In general, yes, though exact formats may vary by event and venue. A healthier civilization should include children in public life, not hide the future from them. Some gatherings may be especially welcoming to families, while others may be better suited to older participants depending on the setting and format.

Do I need talent in singing or movement to participate?
No. The point is not performance perfection. The point is participation, openness, learning, and rediscovering capacities that many people have been taught to suppress, fear, or leave behind.

Is the show free?
Yes. All World Amazing Shows are free.

Why are the shows free?
Because the purpose is public life, not gatekeeping. A real public show about making life amazing for all should not be built around pricing people out of the room.

Can I help bring a show to my area?
Yes. If you have a group, venue, community, or local network that wants to host a World Amazing gathering, email hello@worldamazing.org.

What if I want to go deeper after attending?
You can keep reading the plan, share the work with others, stay connected for future gatherings, start conversations in your own community, or help bring a show to your area. The point is not only to attend something once. It is to help life move in a better direction.
